A decision tree is a flowchart-like tree structure used for both classification and regression tasks. It makes decisions based on feature values and splits data recursively until a prediction is made.

  • Medical diagnosis (e.g., predicting disease based on symptoms)
  • Credit scoring and risk analysis
  • Customer segmentation and targeting
  • Loan approval systems
  • Business decision modeling

üå≤ Types of Decision Trees

  • Classification Tree: Used when the target is categorical (e.g., "Yes" or "No")
  • Regression Tree: Used when the target is numerical (e.g., price or age)

‚öôÔ∏è How a Decision Tree Works

The tree starts from a root node and splits data at decision nodes based on feature values. It continues splitting until it reaches a leaf node, which holds the final prediction.

Key concepts used to split nodes:

  • Gini Impurity: Measures how often a randomly chosen element would be incorrectly labeled
  • Entropy: Measures information gain (used in ID3 algorithm)
  • Information Gain: Gain in purity after a split
